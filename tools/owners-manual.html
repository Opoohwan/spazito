If the bot goes dark

If someone keeps trying to break in, the bot seals itself and stops answering everyone — including you. That's deliberate. A bot that goes quiet under attack is safer than one that keeps talking.

You'll get one text telling you it happened. To bring it back, text:

unlock your-unlock-phrase

Case-sensitive — type it exactly. And the phrase alone isn't enough: it only works from your phone.

Your unlock phrase — keep private

Curious what it's been fending off? Text log for recent blocked attempts. Senders show as scrambled codes, never phone numbers — it keeps nobody's number but yours. It won't pester you about this unless you ask.

If something seems off

No text today? Weekend or market holiday? Before 5pm? Text list — if it answers, it's alive, and it'll tell you if it's paused.

A price says "n/a"? That one ticker didn't answer. The rest still came through on purpose — a partial answer beats none. Usually fixes itself by tomorrow.

Prices look stale? Market was closed. That is the current price.

"Busy — try again"? Two commands collided. Wait a few seconds, resend.

Nothing works at all? Get hold of whoever set this up for you. They built it.

What arrives

Every weekday afternoon, around 5 o'clock, your phone buzzes once:

S&P 749 | Gold 367 | Silver 52.16 [#9 869A94CC]

That's the whole product. No app to open, nothing to log into. It arrives, you glance, you get on with your day. Weekends off. On market holidays you'll see the last close — the market was shut, so the numbers genuinely haven't moved.

The [#9 869A94CC] isn't junk. It's a signature, and it's yours. See the facing page.

Using it2

Nobody commands it but you

The bot takes orders from your number only. A text from anyone else is ignored completely — no reply, no error, no hint anything is there. It doesn't argue with strangers. It just doesn't answer.

Proving a text is really yours

Anyone can spoof a text message. So every message is signed — and you can check it yourself, offline, with a key only you have. Nothing gets sent anywhere. No internet needed.
